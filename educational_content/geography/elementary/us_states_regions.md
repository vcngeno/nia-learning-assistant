## Important Geographic Features

### Major Rivers:
1. **Mississippi River** - 2nd longest in U.S.
2. **Missouri River** - Longest in U.S.
3. **Colorado River** - Carved the Grand Canyon
4. **Rio Grande** - Border with Mexico
5. **Hudson River** - New York

### Major Mountain Ranges:
1. **Rocky Mountains** - West
2. **Appalachian Mountains** - East
3. **Sierra Nevada** - California
4. **Cascade Range** - Pacific Northwest

### Major Lakes:
1. **Great Lakes** (5 lakes: Huron, Ontario, Michigan, Erie, Superior)
2. **Great Salt Lake** - Utah (saltier than ocean!)

### Oceans/Seas:
1. **Atlantic Ocean** - East coast
2. **Pacific Ocean** - West coast
3. **Gulf of Mexico** - South
4. **Arctic Ocean** - Alaska

## Climate Zones

**Tropical** (Hawaii, Southern Florida)
- Hot and humid year-round
- Lots of rain
- Palm trees and beaches

**Desert** (Southwest)
- Very hot and dry
- Little rain
- Cacti and sparse vegetation

**Temperate** (Much of U.S.)
- Four seasons
- Moderate temperatures
- Varied precipitation

**Continental** (Midwest, Northeast)
- Cold winters, hot summers
- More extreme temperatures
- Distinct seasons

**Arctic** (Alaska)
- Very cold
- Long, dark winters
- Short summers

## Why Geography Matters

Understanding U.S. geography helps you:
1. Know where you live in relation to others
2. Understand different climates and cultures
3. Plan trips and vacations
4. Understand history better
5. Appreciate the diversity of our country
6. Make sense of news and current events

## Key Vocabulary

- **Region**: Area with similar features
- **Climate**: Average weather over time
- **Geography**: Study of Earth's features
- **Plains**: Flat grasslands
- **Coast**: Land along the ocean
- **Mountain range**: Group of connected mountains
- **Territory**: Land controlled by a country but not a state

## Remember:

The United States is HUGE and DIVERSE! From snowy Alaska to tropical Hawaii, from deserts to forests, from mountains to beaches - we have it all! Each region and state has special features that make it unique.
