## The 7 Continents

Think of continents as giant pieces of land. There are 7 continents, from biggest to smallest:

### 1. Asia üèØ
**Size:** Largest continent!

**Location:** Eastern Hemisphere, between Pacific and Indian Oceans

**Countries:** 48 countries including:
- China (most people!)
- India (2nd most people!)
- Japan, Thailand, Vietnam, Saudi Arabia, Iraq

**Population:** Over 4.6 billion people (most populated!)

**Geography:**
- Mount Everest (tallest mountain in world!)
- Himalayas (tallest mountain range)
- Gobi Desert
- Lots of islands (Japan, Philippines, Indonesia)

**Climate:** Everything from tropical to arctic!

**Famous For:**
- The Great Wall of China
- Ancient civilizations
- Pandas, tigers, elephants
- Rice farming
- Technology (Japan, South Korea)
- Spices and silk

**Fun Facts:**
- Home to 60% of world's population!
- Has the world's highest AND lowest points
- Birthplace of all major world religions

### 2. Africa ü¶Å
**Size:** 2nd largest

**Location:** Straddles the equator, between Atlantic and Indian Oceans

**Countries:** 54 countries including:
- Egypt, South Africa, Kenya, Nigeria, Morocco

**Population:** Over 1.3 billion people

**Geography:**
- Sahara Desert (largest hot desert!)
- Nile River (longest river!)
- Congo Rainforest (2nd largest rainforest)
- Mount Kilimanjaro
- Victoria Falls

**Climate:** Mostly hot; tropical rainforests and deserts

**Famous For:**
- Wildlife safaris (lions, elephants, giraffes, zebras)
- Ancient Egypt and pyramids
- Diamonds and gold
- Diverse cultures and languages
- Birthplace of humanity

**Fun Facts:**
- Has the longest river (Nile) AND largest desert (Sahara)!
- Home to over 2,000 languages
- The equator runs through the middle

### 3. North America üóΩ
**Size:** 3rd largest

**Location:** Western Hemisphere, between Atlantic and Pacific Oceans

**Countries:** 23 countries including:
- United States, Canada, Mexico

**Population:** Over 590 million people

**Geography:**
- Rocky Mountains
- Grand Canyon
- Mississippi River
- Great Lakes
- Niagara Falls
- Arctic tundra to tropical beaches

**Climate:** Varies greatly - arctic cold to tropical heat

**Famous For:**
- Statue of Liberty
- Hollywood
- Niagara Falls
- Maple syrup (Canada)
- Ancient Mayan ruins (Mexico)
- Baseball, basketball, American football

**Fun Facts:**
- Home to the longest cave system (Mammoth Cave)
- Canada is the 2nd largest country in world
- Has every type of climate!

### 4. South America ü¶ú
**Size:** 4th largest

**Location:** Western Hemisphere, mostly below the equator

**Countries:** 12 countries including:
- Brazil, Argentina, Peru, Colombia, Chile

**Population:** Over 430 million people

**Geography:**
- Amazon Rainforest (largest rainforest!)
- Amazon River (2nd longest river!)
- Andes Mountains (longest mountain range!)
- Angel Falls (highest waterfall!)
- Atacama Desert (driest desert!)

**Climate:** Mostly tropical and warm

**Famous For:**
- Amazon Rainforest (produces 20% of world's oxygen!)
- Machu Picchu (ancient Inca city)
- Soccer/football
- Carnival (Brazil)
- Coffee
- Tango dancing (Argentina)
- Amazing biodiversity

**Fun Facts:**
- Portuguese is spoken in Brazil; Spanish in most other countries
- Home to anacondas, jaguars, piranhas
- Has the world's highest waterfall!

### 5. Antarctica üêß
**Size:** 5th largest

**Location:** The South Pole!

**Countries:** NO countries! No permanent residents!

**Population:** 0 permanent residents (only scientists visiting)

**Geography:**
- Entirely covered in ice!
- Ice sheet is 1-3 miles thick!
- Contains 90% of world's ice
- No trees or bushes
- Surrounded by Southern Ocean

**Climate:** Coldest place on Earth!
- Average temperature: -49¬∞F
- Coldest ever recorded: -128.6¬∞F
- Strong winds

**Famous For:**
- Penguins!
- Scientific research stations
- Icebergs
- No permanent human population
- Untouched wilderness

**Fun Facts:**
- It never rains, but it snows!
- Has no government or cities
- Sun doesn't set for 6 months, then doesn't rise for 6 months!
- If all the ice melted, oceans would rise 200 feet!

### 6. Europe üè∞
**Size:** 6th largest (2nd smallest)

**Location:** Eastern Hemisphere, west of Asia

**Countries:** 44 countries including:
- United Kingdom, France, Germany, Italy, Spain, Russia (part)

**Population:** Over 740 million people

**Geography:**
- Alps Mountains
- Many peninsulas and islands
- Many rivers (Rhine, Danube)
- Mix of plains and mountains
- Very jagged coastline

**Climate:** Mostly temperate; cold in north, warm in south

**Famous For:**
- Ancient Greece and Rome
- Eiffel Tower (France)
- Colosseum (Italy)
- Big Ben (England)
- Art and museums
- Castles
- Different languages and cultures

**Fun Facts:**
- Vatican City is the smallest country in the world!
- Has over 200 languages spoken
- Most developed continent
- Birthplace of Renaissance

### 7. Australia/Oceania ü¶ò
**Size:** Smallest continent!

**Location:** Southern Hemisphere, between Pacific and Indian Oceans

**Countries:** Australia, New Zealand, Papua New Guinea, + island nations

**Population:** Over 40 million people (least populated!)

**Geography:**
- Mostly flat and dry
- Great Barrier Reef (largest coral reef!)
- Uluru/Ayers Rock
- Many small island nations
- Deserts in center (Outback)

**Climate:** Varies; hot/dry interior, tropical north, temperate coasts

**Famous For:**
- Unique animals (kangaroos, koalas, platypuses!)
- Great Barrier Reef
- Sydney Opera House
- Aboriginal culture (oldest continuous culture!)
- Surfing and beaches
- Kiwis (bird and fruit!)

**Fun Facts:**
- Australia is the only country that's also a continent!
- Has the most venomous snakes and spiders
- Kangaroos outnumber people in Australia!
- No active volcanoes on Australian mainland

## The 5 Oceans

Oceans are HUGE bodies of salt water that cover most of Earth!

### 1. Pacific Ocean üåä
**Size:** BIGGEST! (Larger than all continents combined!)

**Location:** Between Asia/Australia and Americas

**Facts:**
- Deepest ocean (Mariana Trench: 36,000 feet deep!)
- Has the Ring of Fire (volcanoes and earthquakes)
- Name means "peaceful" (but can have big storms!)
- Has over 25,000 islands

**Marine Life:** Whales, dolphins, sharks, sea turtles

### 2. Atlantic Ocean üö¢
**Size:** 2nd largest

**Location:** Between Americas and Europe/Africa

**Facts:**
- S-shaped
- Bermuda Triangle is here
- Important for shipping between continents
- Mid-Atlantic Ridge (underwater mountain range)

**Marine Life:** Whales, fish, sea birds

### 3. Indian Ocean üèùÔ∏è
**Size:** 3rd largest

**Location:** Between Africa, Asia, and Australia

**Facts:**
- Warmest ocean
- Has many islands (Madagascar, Maldives, Seychelles)
- Important trade routes
- Monsoons affect this ocean

**Marine Life:** Tropical fish, coral reefs, dolphins

### 4. Southern Ocean (Antarctic Ocean) üßä
**Size:** 4th largest

**Location:** Surrounds Antarctica

**Facts:**
- Coldest ocean
- Newest ocean (named in 2000)
- Very rough seas
- Lots of icebergs

**Marine Life:** Penguins, seals, whales, krill

### 5. Arctic Ocean ‚ùÑÔ∏è
**Size:** Smallest ocean

**Location:** North Pole, surrounded by North America, Europe, Asia

**Facts:**
- Coldest ocean (frozen most of year!)
- Smallest and shallowest
- Home to polar bears
- Ice is melting due to climate change

**Marine Life:** Polar bears, seals, walruses, arctic fish

## The Equator üåê

**What it is:** Imaginary line around middle of Earth

**Location:** 0¬∞ latitude

**Divides Earth into:**
- Northern Hemisphere (north of equator)
- Southern Hemisphere (south of equator)

**Special features:**
- Hottest part of Earth
- Day and night are always about 12 hours each
- Runs through 13 countries

**Countries on the equator:**
- Ecuador (named after it!), Colombia, Brazil
- Democratic Republic of Congo, Kenya
- Indonesia

## The Prime Meridian üìç

**What it is:** Imaginary line running from North to South Pole

**Location:** 0¬∞ longitude, runs through Greenwich, England

**Divides Earth into:**
- Eastern Hemisphere (east of Prime Meridian)
- Western Hemisphere (west of Prime Meridian)

## Hemispheres

Earth can be divided in different ways:

**Northern/Southern Hemispheres:**
- Divided by Equator
- More land in Northern
- More water in Southern

**Eastern/Western Hemispheres:**
- Divided by Prime Meridian
- Most of Americas in Western
- Europe, Africa, Asia, Australia in Eastern

## Important Lines on Earth

**Tropic of Cancer:** 23.5¬∞N (Northern tropical zone)
**Tropic of Capricorn:** 23.5¬∞S (Southern tropical zone)
**Arctic Circle:** 66.5¬∞N (near North Pole)
**Antarctic Circle:** 66.5¬∞S (near South Pole)

## Key Vocabulary

- **Continent**: Large landmass
- **Ocean**: Large body of salt water
- **Hemisphere**: Half of Earth
- **Equator**: Imaginary line around Earth's middle
- **Prime Meridian**: Imaginary line from pole to pole
- **Latitude**: Distance north/south of equator
- **Longitude**: Distance east/west of Prime Meridian
